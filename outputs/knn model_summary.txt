AMAZON USER SEGMENTATION - K-MEANS MODEL SUMMARY

--------------------------------------------------

Project Overview:
This project performs customer segmentation on Amazon users using the K-Means Clustering algorithm. The objective is to group users based on their Annual Income and Purchase Rating patterns to identify meaningful customer segments.

--------------------------------------------------

Algorithm Used:
K-Means Clustering

--------------------------------------------------

Features Used:
1. Annual Income in INR
2. Purchase Rating

--------------------------------------------------

Model Workflow:
1. Imported and cleaned dataset
2. Selected important customer features
3. Applied Elbow Method to determine optimal number of clusters
4. Trained K-Means clustering model
5. Assigned cluster labels to each customer
6. Visualized segmented customer groups

--------------------------------------------------

Optimal Number of Clusters:
4

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster Interpretation:

Cluster 1 (Pink):
Customers with medium income and average purchase ratings.
Represents regular and stable users.

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster 2 (Blue):
Customers with high income and mixed purchase ratings.
Represents premium users with varying engagement levels.

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster 3 (Red):
Customers with low income but diverse purchase ratings.
This cluster may include budget-conscious users.

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster 4 (Cyan):
Customers with high purchase ratings and moderate-to-high income.
Represents highly engaged and valuable customers.

--------------------------------------------------

Centroids:
The black dots in the visualization represent cluster centroids.
Centroids indicate the center point of each customer segment.

--------------------------------------------------

Visualization Details:
X-Axis  : Annual Income in INR
Y-Axis  : Purchase Rating

Different colors represent different customer groups generated by the clustering algorithm.
