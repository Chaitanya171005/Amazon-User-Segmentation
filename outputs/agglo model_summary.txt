AMAZON USER SEGMENTATION - CLUSTER SUMMARY

Model Used:
Agglomerative Clustering

Number of Clusters:
6

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster 1:
Users with low ratings and moderate age group.
These users may represent less satisfied customers or inactive buyers.

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster 2:
Young and middle-age users with very high purchase ratings.
This cluster represents highly satisfied and engaged customers.

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster 3:
Users with average ratings and balanced age distribution.
These customers show moderate engagement with the platform.

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster 4:
Young users with medium ratings.
This segment may represent occasional or seasonal shoppers.

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster 5:
Older users with medium-to-high ratings.
These customers may be loyal premium buyers with consistent purchasing behaviour.

--------------------------------------------------

Cluster 6:
Middle-age users with average ratings.
This group reflects regular users with stable purchasing patterns.

--------------------------------------------------

Business Insights:
- High-rating clusters can be targeted for premium memberships and loyalty rewards.
- Low-rating users require personalized offers and service improvement.
- Customer segmentation helps in targeted marketing and recommendation systems.
- Different user groups can help Amazon improve customer retention strategies.

Conclusion:
The clustering model successfully identified distinct customer groups based on age and purchase rating. These insights can help businesses understand customer behaviour and improve decision-making.
